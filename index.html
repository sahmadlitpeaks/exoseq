FAQ

Common questions from clinicians

What is the typical turnaround time for an ExoSeq® report?
Standard reports are delivered within 4–6 weeks of sample receipt. Urgent cases — including critically ill neonates — are accommodated through our Rapid Exome pathway with prioritized review.
Which sample types do you accept?
Whole blood (EDTA), buccal swabs, saliva (with collection kit), and extracted DNA meeting our QC thresholds. Pediatric and trio testing is fully supported with documented chain-of-custody.
How are variants classified and reported?
All variants follow the ACMG/AMP 2015 framework with specialty-specific refinements. Reports clearly separate primary findings, secondary findings (if consented), and variants of uncertain significance with phenotype correlation.
Is genetic counseling included?
Optional pre- and post-test counseling is available in multiple languages. Counselors collaborate directly with prescribing clinicians to ensure consistent communication with the patient.
Do you support reanalysis as new evidence emerges?
Yes. ExoSeq® performs systematic reanalysis on a defined schedule and on clinician request when the phenotype evolves or new disease–gene relationships are published.
